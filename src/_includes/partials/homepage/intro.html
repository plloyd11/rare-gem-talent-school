Addressing a need

Rare Gem Talent School

In Kenya, many of the students with Dyslexia are not given the academic accommodations they need to succeed. They are often told to “work harder” by teachers that have never heard of learning disabilities, or they have teachers who do not believe Dyslexia is a real diagnosis. As a result, many Dyslexic students face expulsion, or simply quit their education, before they enter high school.

After helping their Dyslexic sons earn their diplomas and continue their educations at a University, Nancy Munyi and Phyllis Kariuki took up the challenge of starting a school that would help other Dyslexic students succeed. The Rare Gem Talent School was conceived of and founded in 2012 - Nancy and Phyllis picked the name “Rare Gem” as a metaphor to describe the extremely talented Dyslexic students that are often overlooked by conventional schools. The school began in a rented three-bedroomed house that the founders struggled to transform into an educational space that would positively impact the lives of the first 10 children who made up the student body.
