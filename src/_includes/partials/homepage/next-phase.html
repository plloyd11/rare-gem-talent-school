The next phase

A growing institution

By the start of the 2015 school year, Rare Gem was the home to a total of 62 students and it had moved to its current location - A former motel that the school’s founders have slowly upgraded. The school became more diverse by including students who had different types of learning difficulties like Autism and mild Intellectual Disabilities. Through its team of literacy teachers trained in the Orton-Gillingham Approach, and with the school’s highly motivated directors, Rare Gem created Individual Education Programs (IEP) like those used in the United States. These programs are tailor made to the various learning difficulties experienced by each individual student.

As of the start of the 2020 academic year, 201 students from all over Kenya were registered at the Rare Gem Talent School. The school’s growth proves there is a definite need for the supports offered by the staff, and it also shows the commendable work of the school’s founders. But now the Rare Gem Talent School faces a dire problem.

The school desperately needs more classroom space and larger dormitories, an updated computer lab, and other upgrades to its facilities that are not possible at its current location. The founders of the Rare Gem Talent School are planning to build a new school that will more then double the number of students it can serve. The new school complex will be built on land owned by Rare Gem which will insure the school’s long term viability. The creation of this new school might seem like an impossible goal, but with the support of people like you, we can give more Kenyan students with learning challenges the opportunity to live up to their fullest potential.
